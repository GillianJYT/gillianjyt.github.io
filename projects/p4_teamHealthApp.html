Team Health App (2022)

Overview

This app was the term project for the course MSCI 342: Principles of Software Engineering. We worked as a group of 6 for 4 months. The app is developed using Ruby on Rails. Throughout the 4 months, proper SDLC and Agile methodology was followed: interviewed and assessed client needs, divided our project into sprints, held weekly stand-ups, followed Git best practices, conducted testing, and delivered our product to the client.

My Contribution and Takeaways

Each sprint, there were tasks or features to complete. I added features to the app, made stylistic UX changes, and fixed bugs. Nearing the end of the project, I conducted testing and documented found bugs, and did bug fixes for those found by others. Throughout the whole project, I also helped team members in need. For example, helping them understand errors in the terminal or providing suggestions on how to implement a feature.

Possibilities of Further Development

This app was delivered to the client who was pleased with the app and its functionalities.

If given more time for development, I would love to implement features that we missed out on. We had some 'nice-to-have' and 'impossible' features that weren't considered for development given the time constraint or our lack of skills at the time.
